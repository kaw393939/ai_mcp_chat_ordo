# Chapter 1 — Content Strategy Foundations: Planning What to Say

## Abstract

Content strategy answers three questions: what content do we need, who is it for, and how will it be maintained? This chapter covers Kristina Halvorson's *Content Strategy for the Web* and the discipline of treating content as a business asset.

---

## Kristina Halvorson and Content Strategy (2009)

**Kristina Halvorson** published *Content Strategy for the Web* in 2009, establishing content strategy as a professional discipline. Her core argument: most websites have too much content, most of it is bad, and nobody is responsible for it.

Her framework:

- **Content substance**: what content do we need and why?
- **Content structure**: how is it organized and formatted?
- **Content workflow**: who creates, reviews, and publishes?
- **Content governance**: what are the standards and who enforces them?

**What frustrated her:** Websites where content was created once and never maintained, accumulating like sediment until the site became an archaeological dig.

## Chapter Checklist

- Do you have a documented content strategy?
- Is there a named content owner for every section of your product?
- When was your content last audited for accuracy and relevance?
