# Chapter 9 — AI-Augmented Analytics: From Reporting to Prediction

## Abstract

AI transforms analytics from backward-looking (what happened?) to forward-looking (what will happen?). This chapter covers AI-powered anomaly detection, predictive modeling, natural language querying, and the intelligence concentration thesis applied to data.

## AI Analytics Applications

- **Anomaly detection**: automatically flag unusual patterns (traffic spikes, conversion drops)
- **Predictive models**: forecast churn, revenue, demand
- **Natural language querying**: "Show me last week's conversion rate by channel" → SQL generation
- **Automated insights**: AI identifies the most important trends in the data

## The Intelligence Concentration Thesis

Broad analytics (tracking everything) produces noise. Concentrated analytics (tracking the 5 metrics that drive decisions, with deep drill-down capability) produces intelligence. AI helps by filtering noise and surfacing the signal — but only when given clear business context about what "signal" means.

## Chapter Checklist

- Are you using AI for anomaly detection?
- Can non-technical stakeholders query data in natural language?
- Is your analytics focused on the 5 metrics that drive decisions, not the 50 that look impressive?
