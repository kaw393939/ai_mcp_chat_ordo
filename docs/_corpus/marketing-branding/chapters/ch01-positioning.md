# Chapter 1 — Positioning: Owning a Word in the Mind

## Abstract

Positioning is the discipline of defining how your product is perceived relative to alternatives. This chapter covers Al Ries and Jack Trout's *Positioning*, the concept of category creation, and the modern challenge of positioning in AI-disrupted markets.

---

## Al Ries and Jack Trout: Positioning (1981)

**Al Ries** and **Jack Trout** published *Positioning: The Battle for Your Mind* in 1981, arguing that marketing is not about the product — it is about the *prospect's mind*. The goal is to own a word or concept in the customer's mental map.

Examples: Volvo owns "safety." FedEx owns "overnight." Google owns "search." When a brand owns a word, competitors must either challenge that word (expensive and usually futile) or find a different word to own.

**What frustrated them:** Companies that tried to be everything to everyone, owning no concept in anyone's mind.

## Chapter Checklist

- What word does your brand own in the customer's mind?
- Can you articulate your position in one sentence?
- Does every piece of marketing reinforce that position?
