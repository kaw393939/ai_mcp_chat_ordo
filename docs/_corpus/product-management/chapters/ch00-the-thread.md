# Chapter 0 — The Thread: From Instinct to Evidence

## Abstract

Product management is the discipline of deciding *what* to build — and, more importantly, what *not* to build. This book traces the evolution of product thinking from the factory floor through lean startup methodology, outcome-driven roadmaps, and the AI-augmented decision frameworks that define the current moment.

---

> **A note about this book:**
> Books I–IV covered *how* to build (engineering, design, UI, UX). This book covers *what* to build and *why*. The best engineering and design in the world cannot save a product that solves a problem nobody has.

---

## The Arc of Product Management

- **1930s**: Brand management at Procter & Gamble — one person responsible for one product's success
- **1980s**: Software product management emerges — bridging engineering and market needs
- **2000s**: Agile transforms delivery — from waterfall plans to iterative sprints
- **2011**: Lean Startup — hypothesis-driven development, MVP, pivot-or-persevere
- **2010s**: Outcome-driven roadmaps — measuring outcomes, not outputs
- **2020s**: AI-augmented product decisions — using models for competitive analysis, demand forecasting, and hypothesis generation

## Reader Exercise: The One-Sentence Product

Describe your product (or a product you admire) in one sentence using this template: *"[Product name] helps [specific audience] [achieve specific outcome] by [unique mechanism]."* If you cannot fill in all four blanks, the product strategy is unclear.
