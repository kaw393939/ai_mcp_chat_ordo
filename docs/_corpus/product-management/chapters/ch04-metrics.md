# Chapter 4 — Metrics That Matter: OKRs, North Stars, and Vanity Metrics

## Abstract

Not all metrics are created equal. This chapter covers the discipline of choosing metrics that drive decisions: John Doerr's OKRs, the North Star metric concept, and the critical distinction between vanity metrics (they go up and to the right but don't inform decisions) and actionable metrics (they change what you do).

---

## John Doerr and OKRs (1999)

**John Doerr** introduced Objectives and Key Results (OKRs) to Google in 1999, adapted from Andy Grove's system at Intel. OKRs separate *ambition* (Objective) from *measurement* (Key Results):

- **Objective**: qualitative, inspiring, time-bound ("Make onboarding delightful")
- **Key Results**: quantitative, measurable, verifiable ("Reduce onboarding drop-off from 40% to 15%")

Each objective should have 2–5 key results. If all key results are met, the objective is achieved. If the key results are met but the objective doesn't feel achieved, the key results were wrong.

## The North Star Metric

A **North Star metric** is the single metric that best captures the core value your product delivers to customers. Airbnb's is nights booked. Spotify's is time spent listening. Slack's is messages sent within teams.

The North Star is not a revenue metric — it is a *value* metric. Revenue follows value. If the North Star is healthy, revenue will follow. If the North Star declines, no amount of marketing will fix the problem.

## Vanity vs. Actionable Metrics

| Vanity Metric | Why It's Misleading | Actionable Alternative |
| --------------- | -------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Total sign-ups | Always increases, never decreases | Weekly active users |
| Page views | Doesn't indicate engagement | Time on page, scroll depth |
| App downloads | Installed ≠ used | Day-7 retention rate |
| Social followers | Follower count ≠ engagement | Engagement rate per post |

## Chapter Checklist

- Do you have a North Star metric that captures user value?
- Are your OKRs specific and measurable?
- Can you distinguish vanity metrics from actionable metrics in your dashboard?
- Does every team member know the top 3 metrics and their current values?
