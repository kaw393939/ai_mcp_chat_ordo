# Chapter 5 — Stakeholder Management: Leading Without Authority

## Abstract

Product managers have responsibility without authority. They cannot order engineers to build features, designers to change mockups, or executives to approve budgets. This chapter covers the communication frameworks and influence techniques that enable product leadership: stakeholder mapping, the RACI model, and the art of saying no to powerful people.

---

## The Stakeholder Map

Every product decision has stakeholders — people who are affected by the decision or who can affect the decision. Stakeholder mapping identifies:

- **Decision-makers**: people who can approve or block
- **Influencers**: people whose opinion decision-makers listen to
- **Users**: people who will use the product
- **Affected parties**: people who are impacted but have no direct say

Understanding the stakeholder map before proposing changes prevents the most common PM failure: building consensus in the wrong order.

## The RACI Model

- **Responsible**: who does the work
- **Accountable**: who makes the final decision
- **Consulted**: who provides input before the decision
- **Informed**: who is told after the decision

Every major decision should have exactly one "A." Multiple accountable parties means no one is accountable.

## Chapter Checklist

- Can you draw the stakeholder map for your current initiative?
- Does every major decision have exactly one accountable person (RACI)?
- Do you consult stakeholders before making decisions that affect them?
- Can you articulate the priority tradeoffs to non-technical stakeholders?
