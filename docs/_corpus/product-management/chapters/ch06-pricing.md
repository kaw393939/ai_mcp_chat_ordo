# Chapter 6 — Pricing and Monetization: Capturing the Value You Create

## Abstract

Price is not a number — it is a signal. This chapter covers value-based pricing, freemium architecture, and the psychology of pricing anchors. The principle: **price communicates product positioning as powerfully as design does.**

---

## Madhavan Ramanujam and the Monetization Framework (2016)

**Madhavan Ramanujam** (Simon-Kucher) published *Monetizing Innovation*, demonstrating that the most common cause of product failure is not bad engineering — it is building features that customers won't pay for. His recommendation: discuss willingness-to-pay *before* building, not after.

Four monetization failure modes:

1. **Feature shock**: too many features, price too high
2. **Minivation**: valuable features, price too low (leaving money on the table)
3. **Hidden gem**: valuable product, wrong market or wrong packaging
4. **Undead**: product nobody wants at any price

## Pricing Models for Software

| Model | Best For | Risk |
| ------- | ---------- | ------ |
| Flat subscription | Simple products, predictable revenue | No growth leverage |
| Per-seat pricing | Team tools, collaboration products | Discourages adoption |
| Usage-based | API services, infrastructure | Revenue unpredictability |
| Freemium | Consumer products, network effects | Conversion rate may be too low |
| Enterprise custom | High-value, complex products | Long sales cycles |

## Chapter Checklist

- Have you validated willingness-to-pay before setting your price?
- Does your pricing model align with how customers receive value?
- Can you articulate why your price is *not* the lowest in the market?
