# Editorial Review: Chapter 12 — Future Directions

**Reviewer:** Senior Acquisitions Editor  
**Date:** 2026-03-03  
**File:** `docs/book/chapters/ch12-future-directions.md`  
**Word count:** ~750 words (76 lines)

## Critical Issues (must fix before publication)

1. **Chapter is substantially thinner than peers.** At ~76 lines, it is roughly one-third the length of Chapters 10, 11, or 13. Reads more like an epilogue appendix than a full chapter. Creates a structural letdown.

## Substantive Concerns (strongly recommend fixing)

1. **Three-Horizon View is entirely assertion.** Every bullet is a prediction with no evidence, mechanism, or citation. The chapter warns against *"hype that ignores operational constraints"* but the predictions section does exactly that.

2. **Duplicate sections.** "Additional Evidence" restates "Repository Example" in different words. Merge or cut one.

3. **"Intent compilers" introduced without definition.** Novel term appears nowhere else in the book. Needs a parenthetical definition or citation to prior art.

4. **No model note.** Every other chapter has one. Its absence breaks the pattern.

5. **Strategic Recommendations are generic.** All four could appear in any AI blog post. Tie each to a specific chapter or artifact from the book.

## Minor Issues (copyedit level)

1. Inconsistent forecast confidence: Abstract uses "likely," Three-Horizon uses no hedging.
2. Orphaned closing line after Diagram Prompt.
3. Missing transition between Additional Evidence and Strategic Recommendations.

## Strengths (what works well)

- "Two traps" framing (hype vs. conservatism) sets the right tone.
- Exercise (one-year roadmap with measurable signals) is concrete and actionable.
- Checklist question about preserving human governance role is well-placed.
