# Chapter 3 — Structured Content: Content Models and Headless CMS

## Abstract
Structured content separates *what* content says from *how* it's presented. This chapter covers content modeling, headless CMS architecture, and the principle that content should be created once and rendered everywhere.

## The COPE Principle
**Create Once, Publish Everywhere** — content should be structured so that the same content can appear on a website, a mobile app, a newsletter, and a social post without being rewritten.

## Chapter Checklist
- Is your content structured (fields, types, relationships) or flat (big text blobs)?
- Can the same content appear in multiple contexts without rewriting?
- Do you have content models documented for your key content types?
