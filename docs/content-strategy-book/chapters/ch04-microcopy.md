# Chapter 4 — Microcopy and UX Writing: Words as Interface

## Abstract
Microcopy is the text that lives *in* the interface: button labels, error messages, tooltips, empty states, confirmation dialogs. This chapter covers the discipline of treating every word in the interface as a design decision.

## Key Principles
- **Clarity over cleverness**: "Save changes?" is better than "Commit modifications?"
- **Actionable errors**: "Email address is required" is better than "Invalid input"
- **Consistent verbs**: if buttons say "Save," don't also use "Submit," "Apply," and "Update" for the same action

## Chapter Checklist
- Are your error messages specific and actionable?
- Is your button labeling consistent across the product?
- Have error messages been reviewed by a UX writer (not just an engineer)?
