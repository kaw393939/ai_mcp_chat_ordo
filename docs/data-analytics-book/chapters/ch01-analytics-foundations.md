# Chapter 1 — Analytics Foundations: Measuring What Matters

## Abstract
Analytics begins with a question: what do we need to know, and why? This chapter covers Avinash Kaushik's analytics framework, the distinction between reporting and analysis, and the discipline of asking the right questions before instrumenting anything.

---

## Avinash Kaushik and Web Analytics 2.0 (2009)

**Avinash Kaushik** (Google) published *Web Analytics 2.0*, arguing that most analytics practice is *reporting* (presenting numbers) rather than *analysis* (extracting actionable insight from numbers).

His framework: every analytics question should be answerable by: **"So what?"** If a metric goes up, so what? What should we *do* differently? If the answer is "nothing," the metric is vanity. If the answer changes a decision, the metric is actionable.

**What frustrated him:** Dashboards with a hundred metrics and zero insights.

## Chapter Checklist
- Does every metric in your dashboard change a decision?
- Can you articulate the "so what?" for your top 5 metrics?
- Are you reporting or analyzing?
