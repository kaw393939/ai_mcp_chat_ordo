# Chapter 4 — Funnels and Cohorts: Understanding User Behavior Over Time

## Abstract
Funnel analysis shows where users drop off in a sequence. Cohort analysis shows how user behavior changes over time. Together, they answer: where do we lose users, and do our changes actually improve retention?

## Funnel Analysis
Every user journey is a funnel: visitors → sign-ups → activations → retained users → paying users → advocates. Each step has a conversion rate. The lowest conversion rate (the "leak") is where intervention creates the most impact.

## Cohort Analysis
A cohort is a group of users who share a common starting point (e.g., signed up in January). Tracking cohorts over time reveals whether product changes are improving retention or just masking churn with new sign-ups.

## Chapter Checklist
- Can you identify the biggest "leak" in your primary funnel?
- Are you tracking cohort retention, not just aggregate metrics?
- Has your Day-7 retention improved over the last 3 months?
