# Chapter 2 — Validation: Testing Before Building

## Abstract
The most expensive mistake is building something nobody wants. This chapter covers Steve Blank's customer development, the Mom Test for honest feedback, and the discipline of pre-selling before engineering.

---

## Steve Blank and Customer Development (2005)

**Steve Blank** published *The Four Steps to the Epiphany*, arguing that startups fail not because of engineering failures but because of *customer development* failures — they never verified that customers existed for their product.

His four-step process:
1. **Customer Discovery**: find and understand potential customers
2. **Customer Validation**: prove that customers will *pay*
3. **Customer Creation**: build demand through marketing
4. **Company Building**: scale the organization

## Rob Fitzpatrick and the Mom Test (2013)

**Rob Fitzpatrick** identified the core problem with customer interviews: people lie. Not maliciously — out of politeness. They say your idea is great because they don't want to hurt your feelings.

The Mom Test: ask questions that even your mom can't lie about:
- ❌ "Would you use a product that does X?" (they'll say yes to be nice)
- ✅ "Tell me about the last time you dealt with X" (they can't fake a specific story)
- ✅ "How are you solving X today?" (reveals real behavior, not hypothetical intent)
- ✅ "How much time/money are you currently spending on X?" (quantifies the pain)

## Chapter Checklist
- Have you validated demand *before* building the product?
- Are your customer interviews using the Mom Test format?
- Have you achieved at least one pre-sale or letter of intent?
