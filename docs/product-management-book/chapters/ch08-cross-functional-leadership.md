# Chapter 8 — Cross-Functional Leadership: Engineering, Design, and Business Alignment

## Abstract
Product management sits at the intersection of engineering, design, and business. This chapter covers the communication frameworks, meeting cadences, and decision-making protocols that keep these three functions aligned without bureaucratic overhead.

---

## The Three-Function Model

Effective product development requires three functions working in concert:
- **Engineering**: determines what is *feasible* (and at what cost)
- **Design**: determines what is *usable* (and what is delightful)
- **Product**: determines what is *valuable* (and what is viable for the business)

When any function dominates, the product suffers: engineering-led products are powerful but unusable, design-led products are beautiful but unfeasible, and business-led products are profitable short-term but beloved by no one.

## The Decision Log

One practice that consistently improves cross-functional alignment: maintaining a publicly visible **decision log** that records every significant product decision, the reasoning behind it, the alternatives considered, and who was accountable.

Decision logs reduce the most common cross-functional failure: relitigating decisions that were already made. When someone asks "why did we do X?", the log answers without requiring a meeting.

## Chapter Checklist
- Does your team have regular cross-functional sync (engineering + design + product)?
- Is there a decision log that captures reasoning, not just outcomes?
- Can every team member articulate the current quarter's top 3 priorities?
- Are disagreements resolved through evidence and frameworks, not authority?
